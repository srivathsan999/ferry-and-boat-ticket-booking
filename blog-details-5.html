Marine Life You Can Spot During Your Journey

February 18, 2023 By Marine Biologist Dr. Emma Ocean

One of the most magical aspects of ferry travel is the opportunity to observe marine life in its natural habitat. From playful dolphins to majestic seabirds, our ferry routes pass through waters teeming with fascinating creatures. Keep your eyes on the ocean, and you might be treated to some incredible wildlife sightings!

Dolphins and Porpoises

Dolphins are among the most commonly spotted marine mammals during ferry journeys. These intelligent creatures are often curious about vessels and may swim alongside the ferry, especially in deeper waters. Look for:

  • Bottlenose Dolphins: Most common, often seen in pods of 5-20 individuals
  • Spinner Dolphins: Known for their acrobatic leaps and spins
  • Harbor Porpoises: Smaller and shyer, usually spotted in calmer waters

Best Time to Spot: Early morning and late afternoon, especially in areas with deeper channels between islands.

Seabirds

Our ferry routes are excellent for bird watching. Common sightings include:

  • Seagulls and Terns: Often following ferries, especially near fishing areas
  • Pelicans: Impressive diving birds, commonly seen near coastal areas
  • Cormorants: Expert divers, often seen drying their wings on rocks
  • Frigatebirds: Magnificent soaring birds with distinctive silhouettes

Whales (Seasonal)

During certain times of the year, particularly in migration seasons (typically spring and fall), lucky passengers may spot whales. Humpback whales, gray whales, and occasionally orcas can be seen in deeper waters. These sightings are rare but unforgettable when they occur. Keep binoculars handy and watch for the telltale spouts of water.

Sea Turtles

In warmer waters, sea turtles are sometimes visible near the surface, especially in areas with seagrass beds. Green turtles and loggerhead turtles are the most commonly spotted species. They often surface to breathe, providing brief but memorable viewing opportunities.

Flying Fish

One of the most entertaining sights is the flying fish, which can glide above the water surface for impressive distances. These fish are most active during daylight hours and are often seen when the ferry disturbs schools of fish near the surface.

"Every ferry journey is a potential wildlife safari. I've seen dolphins, sea turtles, and even a whale on different trips. The ocean is full of life, and ferry travel gives you a front-row seat to nature's wonders."

Dr. Emma Ocean, Marine Biologist

Tips for Wildlife Spotting

To maximize your chances of seeing marine life:

  • Bring binoculars for better viewing
  • Stay on the outer decks, especially the front and sides of the vessel
  • Be patient and scan the water continuously
  • Look for signs like splashes, ripples, or birds diving (indicating fish below)
  • Choose routes that pass through deeper channels and marine protected areas
  • Travel during calm weather conditions for better visibility

Responsible Wildlife Viewing

While observing marine life, remember to be respectful. Our ferries maintain safe distances from wildlife, and we ask passengers not to throw food or objects into the water. Take photos from a distance and enjoy the natural behavior of these amazing creatures without disturbing them.

Best Routes for Wildlife

Some routes offer better wildlife viewing opportunities than others. Routes that pass through deeper channels, near marine reserves, or between less-developed islands typically provide the best chances for sightings. Ask our staff about routes known for frequent wildlife encounters.
