Ferry Safety Tips for New Travelers

May 22, 2023 By Captain Sarah Waters

Traveling by ferry is one of the safest modes of transportation, but being prepared and informed can make your journey even more comfortable and secure. Whether you're a first-time ferry passenger or looking to refresh your knowledge, these essential safety tips will help ensure a smooth and worry-free experience.

Before You Board

Arrive at the terminal at least 30 minutes before departure time. This gives you ample time to check in, find your way around, and locate emergency exits and safety equipment. Always check the weather forecast and ferry schedule updates before leaving home.

  • Keep your ticket and identification easily accessible
  • Wear comfortable, non-slip footwear
  • Pack essential items in a carry-on bag
  • Inform the crew of any medical conditions or mobility needs

Onboard Safety Guidelines

Once onboard, familiarize yourself with the vessel's layout and safety features:

  • Life Jackets: Know the location of life jackets and how to properly wear them
  • Emergency Exits: Identify all emergency exits and evacuation routes
  • Safety Briefing: Pay attention to the crew's safety instructions
  • Stay Seated: Remain seated while the ferry is in motion, especially during rough weather

During the Journey

If you experience motion sickness, sit in the middle of the vessel where movement is minimal. Keep hydrated and avoid heavy meals before traveling. If you feel unwell, inform a crew member immediately. Always hold handrails when moving around the vessel, and keep children supervised at all times.

Weather Considerations

Ferry services may be adjusted or delayed during severe weather conditions. Our vessels are equipped with modern navigation systems and experienced crews trained to handle various weather situations. If you're prone to seasickness, consider taking preventive medication before departure.

"Safety is our top priority. Every ferry in our fleet undergoes regular safety inspections and our crew members are trained in emergency procedures. Your peace of mind is as important as your comfort."

Captain Sarah Waters, Safety Officer

Emergency Procedures

In the unlikely event of an emergency, remain calm and follow crew instructions. All our ferries are equipped with modern safety equipment including life rafts, emergency communication systems, and first aid facilities. Regular safety drills ensure our crew is prepared to assist passengers in any situation.

Remember, ferry travel is statistically one of the safest ways to travel. By following these simple guidelines, you can enjoy a comfortable and secure journey across the water.
