Top 5 Islands to Visit in Summer

June 15, 2023 By Alex Voyager

Summer is the perfect time to explore the hidden gems of our archipelago. The waters are calm, the sun is shining, and the ferries are running on full schedule. Whether you're seeking adventure, relaxation, or cultural experiences, these five islands offer something special for every traveler.

Our carefully curated list features islands that are easily accessible by ferry, each with its own unique charm and attractions. From pristine beaches to vibrant local communities, these destinations promise unforgettable summer memories.

1. Island of Serenity

Known for its crystal-clear turquoise waters and white sandy beaches, Island of Serenity is a paradise for beach lovers. The island features several secluded coves perfect for snorkeling and swimming. Daily ferry services make it easily accessible, with the journey taking approximately 2 hours from the main port. Don't miss the sunset views from the island's highest point!

2. The Blue Lagoon

This stunning island is famous for its natural blue lagoon, formed by a unique combination of coral reefs and underwater springs. The lagoon is perfect for kayaking and paddleboarding. The island also boasts excellent hiking trails that lead to breathtaking viewpoints overlooking the entire archipelago.

3. Coral Paradise

A haven for divers and marine enthusiasts, Coral Paradise offers some of the best snorkeling and diving spots in the region. The island's protected marine reserve is home to colorful coral formations and diverse marine life. Multiple ferry routes connect this island daily.

4. Sunset Bay

As the name suggests, this island is renowned for its spectacular sunsets. The western-facing beaches provide the perfect setting for evening relaxation. The island also features charming beachfront restaurants serving fresh local seafood and traditional cuisine.

5. Adventure Isle

For thrill-seekers, Adventure Isle offers water sports, cliff jumping, and jungle trekking. The island maintains a perfect balance between adventure activities and peaceful nature spots. It's an ideal destination for families and groups looking for diverse experiences.

"The journey is just as important as the destination. Ferry travel allows you to slow down and appreciate the vastness of the ocean while building anticipation for the adventures that await."

Alex Voyager, Travel Writer

Planning Your Visit

All five islands are well-connected with regular ferry services throughout the summer season. We recommend booking your tickets in advance, especially during peak months (July and August). Early morning departures often provide the smoothest crossings and allow you to maximize your time on each island.
